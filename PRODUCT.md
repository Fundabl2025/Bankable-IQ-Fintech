## Product Overview
FundReady is a staged capital-readiness platform designed to help small business owners understand lender expectations, assess their current readiness, improve lender-facing signals, and unlock the right capital products over time.

FORGE is the AI layer that interprets readiness, prioritizes actions, and guides user progression.

---

## Product Thesis
Most businesses are not denied because they are worthless.
They are denied because they are unprepared, mistimed, mismatched to the product, or missing lender-required signals.

FundReady solves that by turning lender-readiness into a visible, guided, measurable progression.

---

## Product Jobs To Be Done

### Functional Jobs
- assess business readiness for funding
- identify lender-relevant weaknesses
- recommend the highest-impact improvements
- match users to appropriate capital products
- help users progress toward bankability
- support coaching, advisory, and done-for-you execution

### Emotional Jobs
- reduce fear of denial
- reduce financial confusion
- increase confidence before applying
- make the user feel guided and supported
- make the process feel fair, understandable, and achievable

### Economic Jobs
- increase approval probability
- increase access to larger capital amounts
- improve capital quality over time
- reduce wasted applications
- reduce unnecessary borrowing cost

## Core User Personas

### 1. First-Time Strategic Applicant (primary)
Has never applied before. Usually bootstrapped or cash flowed the business.
Wants to get funding the right way the first time.
Message: "You have worked too hard to walk into the funding process blind. See how ready your business really is before you apply anywhere."

### 2. Denied but Viable Owner
Has experienced rejection or poor offers. Needs clarity and a recovery path.
Message: "Find out what blocked approval. Fix the gaps that matter most. Turn rejection into a roadmap."

### 3. Growth-Oriented Operator
Needs more sophisticated capital for expansion.
Message: "Graduate to better capital. Unlock larger amounts and better terms."

### 4. Underserved Founder
Needs trust, literacy, and structure.
Message: "Finally understand the rules. Get guided without judgment."

### 5. Advisor / Channel Partner
Needs a repeatable system to guide clients.
Message: "Help clients get funding-ready with a structured system."

### 6. Capital Ecosystem Partner
Needs better-prepared applicants.

**Primary contrast pair for copy:** First-Time Strategic Applicant + Denied but Viable Owner

## Product Journey (6 Stages)

### Stage 1: Discover
User arrives with uncertainty. Needs trust, clarity, and a reason to begin.
Assets: landing page, quiz entry, FundScore promise, no-credit-pull reassurance

### Stage 2: Assess
User completes low-friction readiness assessment.
Output: FundScore, readiness profile, risk/gap summary, product qualification, next-step recommendation

### Stage 3: Understand
User sees how lenders likely view their business.
Output: readiness categories, prioritized gaps, explanation of why each gap matters, product-fit explanation

### Stage 4: Progress
User begins readiness improvement through guided modules.
Assets: compliance modules, action plans, AI coaching, milestone tracking, progress states, prompts

### Stage 5: Unlock
As user improves, new funding pathways become available.
Output: new product matches, updated capital range, readiness progression, application timing guidance

### Stage 6: Convert / Expand
User upgrades to coaching, done-for-you services, or applies through platform partners.

## Core Product Objects

| Object | Purpose |
|---|---|
| FundScore | Top-level readiness indicator (0–1000 scale) |
| Capital Readiness Profile | Deeper diagnostic across lender-relevant categories |
| Gap Stack | Ranked readiness issues ordered by approval impact |
| Capital Path | Staged roadmap showing which actions unlock which outcomes |
| Compliance Modules | 13 structured readiness-building modules |
| Bankable Status Indicator | Progress marker — how close to stronger capital eligibility |
| FORGE Coach | AI guide — explains findings, prioritizes actions, keeps user moving |

## Experience Requirements

### Assessment
Must feel: simple, fast, useful, non-invasive, confidence-building
Must NOT feel: like a loan application, hard sell, credit repair funnel, compliance burden

### Dashboard
Must always show: current readiness, what changed, what matters most, what to do next, what unlocks next

### Coaching
Must feel: smart, practical, non-judgmental, specific, motivating

---

## Scoring Logic Principles
- understandable by non-experts
- tie recommendations to clear rationale
- avoid fake precision
- avoid unsupported guarantees
- distinguish current access from future potential
- separate diagnostic insight from lender decision authority

## Product Non-Negotiables
- never confuse assessment with approval
- never recommend applying without readiness context
- never hide the reason behind a recommendation
- never let the dashboard become static
- never make the product feel predatory
- never optimize conversion at the expense of trust

---

## AI Role — FORGE
Frame FORGE as:
- capital-readiness analyst
- funding-path strategist
- lender-signal interpreter
- compliance and sequencing guide
- progress coach

Do NOT frame FORGE as:
- guaranteed approval engine
- a lender
- a credit bureau
- a legal or tax authority (unless explicitly supported by real workflows)

---

## Strategic Product Framework
Three goals, three stages:

**Goal 1 — Visibility:** Understand current readiness, see current capital options, identify key gaps
**Goal 2 — Bankability:** Structured readiness improvements, compliance modules, profile strengthening
**Goal 3 — Full Capital Stack:** SBA loans, bank lines, equipment financing, larger institutional capital

## Core Market Thesis

FundReady closes that gap at scale.
