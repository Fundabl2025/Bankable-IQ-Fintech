Revenue Based Loan

Flexible financing where repayments adjust based on your revenue performance.

Quick facts
Amount: Typically $10,000 to $5,000,000, usually capped at 10% to 30% of your annual recurring revenue.
Term: Generally 6 to 24 months, determined by how long it takes to reach the repayment cap.
Funding speed: Typically 24 hours to 7 days after linking your accounts.
Payments: Flexible, a fixed percentage of monthly or weekly gross revenue, commonly 5% to 10%.
Rates or cost: Priced as a flat fee or factor rate, typically 1.1x to 1.3x the principal, no compounding interest.
Collateral: Generally unsecured, no physical assets required, though a general business lien or personal guarantee is common.
Prepayment penalty: None, but because pricing is a flat fee, you usually do not save money by paying early unless the lender offers an early payoff discount clause.

What it is
A revenue based loan provides capital that is repaid as a set percentage of your revenue, rather than a fixed installment amount. As your revenue increases or decreases, your payment automatically adjusts. Instead of compounding interest, the total repayment amount is typically set upfront using a flat fee or factor rate, and you repay until you reach the agreed repayment cap.

Why people choose it
Payments adjust with revenue, which can reduce pressure during slower months.
Funding can be fast once accounts are linked for data review and monitoring.
Pricing is usually set upfront with a flat fee or factor rate, no compounding interest.
Often unsecured, meaning no specific physical collateral is required, though a general lien or personal guarantee is common.
No prepayment penalty, and payoff flexibility if an early discount clause exists.

Minimum qualifications
Time in business: Typically at least 6 to 12 months.
Revenue: Usually $10,000 to $25,000 in monthly revenue.
Data access: Ability to link business bank accounts and accounting or payment platforms, such as QuickBooks or Stripe, for real-time monitoring.
Profitability: Not always required, but high gross margins, commonly 50% or higher, are preferred.

Best-fit industries
SaaS and software businesses with recurring subscription revenue and strong margins.
Ecommerce businesses with seasonal spikes or predictable online sales.
Professional services firms with high-volume, digital invoicing.
Subscription box services and similar models with high customer retention.

FAQ
How are payments calculated?
Payments are a fixed percentage of your revenue. If you make $100,000 this month and the remittance rate is 5%, you pay $5,000. If revenue drops to $50,000 next month, your payment automatically drops to $2,500.

How is this different from a merchant advance?
Both can use a percentage of sales, but revenue based loans are typically cheaper and often support diverse revenue streams such as B2B payments and ACH. Merchant advances commonly focus on card swipes and can carry higher fees.

What documentation is required?
Typically the last 3 to 6 months of business bank statements, connection to your payment processor such as Stripe, PayPal, or Square, connection to your accounting software, and basic business tax returns for larger loan amounts
