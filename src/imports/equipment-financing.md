What it is
Loans or leases secured by equipment such as vehicles, heavy machinery, tools, and medical devices. This financing can be used to purchase or refinance equipment, and the equipment secures the financing.

Ideal Use Case
Businesses that need essential equipment and want to preserve cash by financing the asset instead of paying the full cost upfront.

Why people choose it
The asset-backed structure can improve financing access because the equipment reduces lender risk.
It supports a wide range of equipment categories, including trucks and trailers.
It preserves working capital for payroll, inventory, and operations while still acquiring needed assets.

Unique Benefits

Up to 100% financing available.
Designed to cover a wide range of assets, including trucks, trailers, construction equipment, kitchen equipment, medical equipment, and IT equipment.

Minimum and Key Qualifications*
Time in Business: Catalog baseline is 1 year, tiering also references 2+ years for startup or subprime tier
Minimum FICO: Catalog baseline is 550+, tiering ranges from 450 to 750+ depending on tier
Minimum Revenue: Cheat sheet indicates $25k+ monthly revenue in the table for equipment financing
Documentation: Equipment invoice required, and tiering may require bank statements plus tax returns, and may require tax returns or PayNet verification
Tier Guidance:
Tier 1A–2: 700 to 750+ FICO, 3 to 7+ years in business, app-only up to $350k to $500k
Tier 3–4: 600 to 675 FICO, 2+ years in business, up to $200k to $250k
Startup or subprime: 450 to 650 FICO, 2+ years, 20% to 40% down payment, bank statements plus tax returns required
Banking: Business bank account required, no personal accounts for sole proprietors
*Minimum Qualifications do not Guarantee Approval.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can I finance equipment from a private seller? Yes, vendor and private sales are eligible.
Can I lease instead of purchase? Yes, purchase and lease options are available.
How fast can it fund? Funding is typically 3 to 10 days depending on verification and tier requirements.
