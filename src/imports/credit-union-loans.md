Credit Union Loans

Relationship-based financing through credit unions, often designed for borrowers who value local decisioning and longer-term banking relationships.

Quick facts
Amount: Loans typically range from $500 to $50,000. Some specialized or larger credit unions may offer up to $100,000 for highly qualified members.
Term: Most repayment periods fall between 12 and 60 months (1 to 5 years). Some institutions offer extended terms up to 84 months for larger loan amounts.
Funding speed: Approval is often same-day. Once approved, funds are typically deposited into your account within 24 hours to one business day.
Payments: Usually fixed monthly installments covering both principal and interest. Many credit unions offer Skip-A-Pay programs, allowing members to skip one or two payments per year for a small fee.
Rates or cost: National average rates for a 36-month unsecured loan are roughly 10.6% to 10.7%. Federal credit unions have a legal interest rate cap of 18% on most loans. Most do not charge origination fees.
Collateral: Most personal loans, often called signature loans, are unsecured and do not require collateral. Secured options using your savings or a certificate of deposit as collateral may be available for lower rates.
Prepayment penalty: Most credit unions do not charge penalties for early payoff.

What it is
Credit union loans are financing products offered through credit unions. Depending on the credit union, these may include personal loans, business loans, term loans, lines of credit, equipment loans, and other lending products.

Why people choose it
Relationship-driven lending with local servicing and member-focused support.
Predictable monthly payments with terms that commonly range from 1 to 5 years.
Many credit unions do not charge origination fees and allow early payoff without penalties.
Secured options may provide lower rates by using savings or a CD as collateral.
Co-borrowers can improve approval odds or pricing for borrowers with weaker credit.

Minimum qualifications
Membership: You must be a member to receive a loan. Eligibility usually depends on where you live, work, worship, or your employer.
Age and residency: You must be at least 18 years old and typically a U.S. citizen or permanent resident.
Minimum deposit: Joining often requires opening a savings account with a small initial deposit, such as $5.

Credit score: Many credit unions look for 600+, though some may approve lower scores if you have a positive relationship with them.
Debt-to-income ratio: A DTI of 36% or less is ideal, though some allow up to 50%.
Income stability: You generally need steady employment, often requiring at least 6 to 12 months at your current job.

Required documentation typically includes:
Proof of identity: Valid government-issued photo ID such as a driver’s license or passport.
Proof of address: Recent utility bill, mortgage statement, or lease agreement.
Proof of income: Recent pay stubs, W-2s, 1099s, or tax returns, especially if self-employed.
Employment details: Employer name, contact information, and length of employment.

Loan options commonly available:
Unsecured loans: Based on creditworthiness without collateral.
Secured loans: Backed by savings or a certificate of deposit, often with lower rates.
Co-borrowers: Adding a co-applicant can help you qualify or get better terms if credit is low.

Best-fit industries
Individuals, entrepreneurs, and business owners who want relationship-based lending and predictable monthly payments. Availability and appetite vary by credit union and loan type.

FAQ
Do I have to be a member?
Yes, membership is required and eligibility is often based on where you live, work, worship, or your employer.

How fast can I get funded?
Approval is often same-day, and funds are typically deposited within 24 hours to one business day after approval.

What if my credit score is below 600?
Some credit unions may approve lower scores when you have a strong relationship, and adding a co-borrower may improve approval odds or terms.
