SECTION 1 — WHAT THIS PLATFORM IS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FundReady™ is a pre-funding readiness SaaS platform for the 33 million small
businesses in the United States. It exists to solve one problem:

  Small businesses are denied funding not because they're unworthy —
  but because they were never prepared before applying.

The platform functions as three things simultaneously:
  → Virtual CFO         (knows what lenders see)
  → Funding Strategist  (knows which products they qualify for)
  → Action Engine       (tells them exactly what to fix, in what order)

THREE USER GOALS (the platform serves all three in sequence):
  Goal 1: Access as much funding as possible RIGHT NOW
  Goal 2: Build a bankable business over the next 30–90 days
  Goal 3: Access larger, lower-rate conventional/SBA loans long-term

CORE ENGINE — three layers that always work together:
  Layer 1: FundScore™ (0–1000) — 6-dimension weighted composite score
  Layer 2: 20-Item Lender Compliance Checklist — structural bankability
  Layer 3: 17-Product Eligibility Matrix — pre-qualification engine

The user's score + compliance status = their exact eligibility across
17 funding products. Everything on the platform feeds this engine.

── 2A. DESIGN PHILOSOPHY ────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This is a COMMAND CENTER, not a dashboard.
The visual language must communicate:
  1. Precision — every number is real and trustworthy
  2. Urgency — there is always a next move
  3. Authority — this system knows what lenders know
  4. Momentum — progress is always visible

Reference points: Bloomberg Terminal meets fintech meets SaaS clarity.
NOT: colorful startup. NOT: banking conservatism. NOT: generic dashboard.

Dark mode only. No white backgrounds. No light theme toggle in v1.
Every screen must feel like a control room, not a website.

SECTION 7 — DESIGN DECISIONS & RATIONALE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHY DARK MODE ONLY:
  The target user is making serious financial decisions. Dark mode signals
  seriousness, reduces eye strain during long sessions, and creates authority.
  Light dashboards feel like marketing. Dark dashboards feel like tools.

WHY SHARP CORNERS (0 border radius on cards):
  Rounded corners = consumer app. Sharp corners = financial terminal.
  We want to look like Bloomberg, not a consumer fintech app. Sharp corners
  communicate precision and trustworthiness to a skeptical user audience.

WHY LIME AS PRIMARY:
  Green signals money, approval, and success — but lime specifically is
  distinctive (not the generic fintech teal/blue). It stands out against
  the dark bg system without screaming. It says "funded" not "funded.com."

WHY SYNE FOR NUMBERS:
  Syne's geometric structure makes financial numbers feel precise and credible.
  A score of 624 in Syne 800 looks authoritative. In Inter, it looks like a
  settings page. The font choice is doing emotional work.

WHY CRIMSON PRO FOR ADVISORY TEXT:
  Platform voice must be advisory, not robotic. Crimson Pro italic shifts the
  register from dashboard-read to human-guidance. When the platform explains
  WHY something matters, it should feel like a knowledgeable advisor talking
  — not a tooltip firing.

WHY NO TOOLTIPS:
  Tooltips are a crutch for unexplained UI. If a user needs to hover to
  understand something, the UI failed. Every element should be self-explanatory
  or have its explanation inline. Especially for a user who may have limited
  financial literacy — hover-to-understand is a terrible pattern here.

WHY THE COMPLIANCE DOTS:
  20 dots give the user an immediate gestalt of their compliance status.
  They don't need to count. The color ratio tells the story instantly.
  Green-heavy = good. Red-heavy = work to do. This is data visualization
  doing the job of a status summary.

WHY THE SCORE BAND SYSTEM:
  A score of 624 means nothing without context. "Developing — Momentum Building"
  with a teal color means something emotionally. Users need to understand their
  position in a range — not just a number. The band + color + label does this
  in 3 elements simultaneously.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Platform speaks like a CFO who has been in the room with lenders.
Direct. Specific. Never inspirational. Never apologetic.

  No compliance items checked:

  No products unlocked:
    "Complete your assessment to see which products you pre-qualify for today."
    CTA: "Get My Score →"

ERROR STATES:
  "Something went wrong. Your data is safe. Refresh to continue."

LOADING STATES:
  "Analyzing your profile against 17 lender requirements..."
  "Running eligibility matrix..."
  "Building your action plan..."

  0–399:   "Foundational work required before lender conversations."
  400–549: "Foundation is being built. Focus on your top 3 actions."
  550–649: "Momentum is building. You pre-qualify for several products now."
  650–749: "Strong position. Premium products are within reach."

  900+:    "Prime. SBA access, lowest rates, maximum leverage."

SECTION 10 — FINAL INSTRUCTIONS FOR THE DESIGNER
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The user of this platform has been denied funding. They came here because
they were rejected, embarrassed, and confused. They are smart enough to know
they need help. They are skeptical of another tool that promises and under-delivers.

Every design decision must answer: "Does this make the user feel more capable,
more informed, and more in control — or does it make them feel like they're
using a product?"

This is a $10B problem. Design it like one.

Priority order for screens:
  1. Design System (all tokens + components)
  2. Dashboard (the core experience)
  3. Capital Access Map (the most data-dense screen)
  4. FundScore™ Assessment (the entry point)
  5. Action Plan (the operational core)
  6. Landing Page (conversion)
  7. Remaining screens

Build components FIRST. Then build screens from components.
Never build one-off designs. Everything must be reusable.
Every component must have: default · hover · active · disabled · loading · error · empty.

The score, the compliance dots, and the action plan cards are the
three most important visual elements in the entire system.
Get those right above everything else.
