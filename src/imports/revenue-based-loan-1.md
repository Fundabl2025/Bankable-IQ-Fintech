Revenue Based Loan

Product
Revenue Based Loan

Quick Facts
Amount: Typically $10,000 to $5,000,000, commonly capped at 10% to 30% of annual recurring revenue
Term: Generally 6 to 24 months, determined by how long it takes to reach the repayment cap
Funding Speed: Typically 24 hours to 7 days after linking accounts
Payments: Flexible, a fixed percentage of monthly or weekly gross revenue, commonly 5% to 10%
Rates or Cost: Flat fee or factor rate, typically 1.1x to 1.3x principal, no compounding interest
Collateral: Generally unsecured, though a general business lien or personal guarantee is common
Prepayment Penalty: None, but because cost is a flat fee, early payoff usually does not reduce total cost unless the lender offers an early payoff discount clause
Pros: Payments adjust with revenue, fast funding once accounts are linked, no compounding interest, often unsecured
Cons: Total repayment is typically fixed via factor rate, lender may require lien or personal guarantee, high gross margins are commonly preferred

What it is
A financing structure where you receive capital upfront and repay it through a fixed percentage of gross revenue rather than a fixed installment. Because repayments are tied to revenue, the payment amount increases when revenue rises and decreases when revenue drops. The total repayment amount is typically determined upfront using a flat fee or factor rate, and there is no compounding interest.

Ideal Use Case
Businesses with steady revenue flow and strong gross margins that want repayment flexibility, especially companies that experience seasonal swings or fluctuating month-to-month revenue.

Why people choose it
Payments automatically adjust based on revenue, which can reduce strain during slower months.
Funding can be fast when the lender can monitor revenue through linked accounts.
Pricing is typically set upfront, so there is no compounding interest.

Unique Benefits
Repayment is a fixed percentage of revenue, so payments decrease automatically when revenue decreases.
Pricing is based on a flat fee or factor rate, commonly 1.1x to 1.3x, with no compounding interest.

Top Industries by volume
SaaS and software
ECommerce
Professional services with high-volume digital invoicing
Subscription box services and retention-based models

Minimum and Key Qualifications*
Time in Business: Typically 6 to 12 months
Minimum Revenue: Typically $10,000 to $25,000 in monthly revenue
Data Access: Ability to link bank accounts and accounting or payment platforms such as QuickBooks and Stripe for real-time monitoring
Profitability: Not always required, but high gross margins are commonly preferred, often 50%+
*Minimum Qualifications do not Guarantee Approval.

Frequently Asked Questions
How are payments calculated? Payments are a fixed percentage of revenue, if you make $100,000 and your remittance rate is 5%, you pay $5,000, if revenue drops to $50,000, your payment drops to $2,500.
How is this different from a merchant advance? Revenue-based financing is typically cheaper and supports broader revenue streams like B2B and ACH, while merchant advances often focus on card swipes and can carry higher fees.
What documentation is required? Commonly last 3 to 6 months of business bank statements, connection to payment processor such as Stripe, PayPal, or Square, connection to accounting software, and tax returns for larger amounts.
