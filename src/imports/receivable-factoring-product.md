What it is
Receivable factoring, described as accounts receivable finance or an A/R line of credit, is a revolving funding facility secured by outstanding receivables. The available credit can move up or down as receivables change, and it is designed to stabilize cash flow when customers pay slowly.

Ideal Use Case
B2B businesses waiting on slow-paying customers that need reliable working capital to cover payroll, inventory, and operating expenses without waiting for invoices to be paid.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is this for B2B or B2C? It is designed for B2B businesses and is not suitable for B2C, verifiable B2B customers are required.
Why is it called revolving? Because availability can increase or decrease as outstanding receivables fluctuate, and the facility is designed to be reused as receivables are collected and verified.
What are the main constraints? A/R verification is required, the business must have verifiable B2B customers, and clean financial reporting is required.
