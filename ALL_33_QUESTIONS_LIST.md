# FundReady Assessment - Complete List of All 33 Questions

## Overview
- **Foundation Questions (Q_F1–Q_F11):** 11 questions covering owner info, business basics, banking, and assets

## PART 1: FOUNDATION QUESTIONS (Q_F1–Q_F11)

### Q_F1: Owner Contact Information
**Question Title:** "Let's start with your contact information"
**Why:** "We need to know who we're working with. This information helps us personalize your experience and ensures we can reach you with important updates about your fundability."
**Data Collected:** 
- First Name
- Last Name
- Email Address
- Phone Number

### Q_F2: Business Name + Entity Type
**Question Title:** "What's your business legal name and entity type?"
**Why:** "Lenders verify your legal name against state records and EIN databases. Entity type is the #1 structural signal — it determines your legal separation from personal liability."
**Data Collected:**
- Business Legal Name (text)
- Entity Type (select):
  - Sole Proprietorship
  - LLC (Single-Member)
  - LLC (Multi-Member) / Partnership
  - S-Corp or C-Corp

### Q_F3: Business Start Date + Industry Focus
**Question Title:** "When did your business legally start operating, and what is your major industry focus?"
**Why:** "Business age is a hard filter lenders cannot override. Industry determines risk overlay — some industries face automatic additional scrutiny regardless of financials."
**Data Collected:**
- Start Date (Month + Year) → Calculates business age in years/months
- Major Industry Focus (select from 35+ options):
  - Accounting, Tax Preparation, Bookkeeping
  - Advertising, Marketing, Public Relations
  - Agriculture, Farming, Ranching
  - Architecture, Engineering, Surveying
  - Automotive Sales, Service, Repair
  - [... 30+ more options ...]

### Q_F4: EIN + Business Website
**Question Title:** "What is your EIN, and do you have a business website?"
**Why:** "EIN is required for SBA and most institutional lenders — it separates business from personal tax identity. A verifiable web presence is one of the first things lenders check for legitimacy."
**Data Collected:**
- Has EIN? (Yes/No)
- EIN Number (text, optional)
- Has Website? (Yes/No)
- Website URL (text, optional)

### Q_F5: Monthly Revenue + Credit Card Sales
**Question Title:** "What is your average monthly revenue, and do you accept credit cards?"
**Why:** "Monthly revenue sets your maximum borrowing power. Credit card sales are the primary qualification factor for Merchant Cash Advance — the fastest-funding product in the market."
**Data Collected:**
- Average Gross Monthly Revenue (button select):
  - Under $5,000
  - $5,000–$15,000
  - $15,000–$40,000
  - $40,000–$100,000
  - Over $100,000
- Total Monthly Credit Card Sales (button select):
  - I do not accept credit cards
  - Under $5,000
  - $5,000–$15,000
  - $15,000–$50,000
  - Over $50,000

### Q_F6: Banking Information (3 sub-fields)
**Question Title:** "Tell us about your business banking situation."
**Why:** "Lenders read 12 months of bank statements like a report card. Your account type, age, and average balance are verified against stated income. NSFs are flagged automatically."
**Data Collected:**
- Bank Account Type (select):
  - Dedicated Business Account
  - Personal Account for Business
  - No Bank Account
- Account Age (conditional, shows only if account selected):
  - 0–6 months
  - 6–12 months
  - 12–24 months
  - 24+ months
- Average Daily Balance (conditional, shows only if account selected):
  - Near zero
  - $500–$2K
  - $2K–$10K
  - $10K–$25K
  - $25K+

### Q_F7: NSF Events + Business Assets
**Question Title:** "Any overdrafts or NSFs in the last 12 months? And what other business assets do you have?"
**Why:** "NSF events are flagged in lender reviews — even 2–3 per year changes your story. Outstanding invoices, equipment, and purchase orders each unlock different funding products."
**Data Collected:**
- NSF/Overdraft Events in Last 12 Months (select):
  - Zero
  - 1–2
  - 3–5
  - 5+
- Own Real Estate Property? (Yes/No/Planning to acquire)
- Equipment Value (slider, text field)
- Outstanding Invoices (slider, text field)

### Q_F8: Owner Personal Credit Scores (all 3 at once)
**Question Title:** "What are your personal credit scores?"
**Why:** "[Three credit scores combined into one question for efficiency]"
**Data Collected:**
- Personal FICO Score (from credit bureau)
- Vantage Score (alternative if not available)
- Experian/Equifax Alternative Score
- Any/All of the above

### Q_F9: Loan History + Willingness to Pledge Assets
**Question Title:** "Have you successfully repaid any business loans? And would you consider putting up personal or business assets as collateral?"
**Why:** "Past repayment is the strongest behavioral predictor. Collateral willingness changes the entire product roadmap — secured lending has much higher approval rates."
**Data Collected:**
- Past Business Loan Repayment (Yes/No/No defaults)
- Willing to Pledge Personal Assets (Yes/No/Unsure)
- Willing to Pledge Business Assets (Yes/No/Unsure)

### Q_F10: All 3 Credit Scores (Detailed)
**Question Title:** "Let's get your credit scores — we need personal FICO, Vantage, and any business credit data."
**Why:** "Credit scores are the language lenders speak. Three separate scores (personal FICO, Vantage, business credit) tell completely different parts of your story."
**Data Collected:**
- FICO Score (0–850)
- Vantage Score (300–850)
- Business Credit Score/Paydex (if applicable)
- Source of scores (pulled from bureau vs. self-reported)

### Q_F11: Overall Business Readiness (Self-assessment)
**Question Title:** "How ready do you feel your business is for funding?"
**Why:** "Self-perception matters for product placement. Someone who says 'I know my numbers' gets different messaging than someone who says 'I'm not sure where we stand.'"
**Data Collected:**
- Perceived Readiness (scale or select):
  - Very ready — numbers are solid
  - Somewhat ready — most data is prepared
  - Not ready yet — still gathering documents

## PART 2: READINESS QUESTIONS (Q_R1–Q_R23)

### Q_R1: Business Tax Returns Filed
**Question:** "How many years of filed business tax returns do you have?"
**Why:** "The SBA requires 2 years of filed returns. Most banks want 2–3 years. No returns = instant denial from most conventional lenders — no workaround exists."
**Options:**
- None filed yet (Score: D=0.0)
- 1 year filed (Score: D=0.35)
- 2 years filed (Score: D=0.75)
- 3 or more years (Score: D=1.0)

### Q_R2: Profit & Loss Statement
**Question:** "Do you have a current Profit & Loss (P&L) statement?"
**Why:** "A P&L shows lenders whether the business actually makes money. Without one, you're asking them to trust a number with no proof."
**Options:**
- Yes — professionally prepared (CPA or accounting software) (Score: D=1.0)
- Yes — I made it myself in a spreadsheet (Score: D=0.6)
- I have one but it's over 6 months old (Score: D=0.35)
- No P&L (Score: D=0.0)

### Q_R3: Business Bank Statements
**Question:** "How many months of business bank statements can you provide right now?"
**Why:** "Lenders read every line of 12 months of statements. Less than 3 months disqualifies you from most products."
**Options:**
- Less than 3 months (Score: D=0.1)
- 3–5 months (Score: D=0.35)
- 6–11 months (Score: D=0.65)
- 12 or more months (Score: D=1.0)

### Q_R4: Documentation Consistency (Tax Returns vs. Bank Statements)
**Question:** "Do the revenue figures on your tax returns and bank statements roughly match each other?"

**Options:**
- Yes — within 10% (Score: D=1.0)
- Within 15–25% difference (Score: D=0.6)
- Significant difference exists (Score: D=0.15)
- I'm not sure (Score: D=0.35)

### Q_R5: Revenue Trend (Growing/Flat/Declining)
**Question:** "Has your revenue been growing, flat, or declining over the past 12 months?"
**Why:** "Lenders don't just look at where you are — they look at where you're headed. A declining trend triggers automatic concern about repayment ability."
**Options:**
- Growing — consistently up month over month (Score: F=1.0)
- Growing — but inconsistently (Score: F=0.7)
- Flat — roughly the same each month (Score: F=0.5)
- Declining — revenue has been going down (Score: F=0.1)

### Q_R6: Monthly Profitability
**Question:** "After all business expenses, does your business consistently generate a monthly profit?"
**Why:** "Profit is how lenders know you can service new debt. A business that breaks even every month has no margin for a new loan payment."
**Options:**
- Yes — profitable every month (Score: F=1.0)
- Most months — occasional break-even (Score: F=0.65)
- About half the time (Score: F=0.3)
- Rarely or not yet (Score: F=0.05)

### Q_R7: Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) - Loan Payment Capacity
**Question:** "If you took on a new monthly loan payment, could your business comfortably cover it?"
**Why:** "This is the DSCR — the most critical cash flow metric. Lenders want to see 25% more income than debt payments."
**Options:**
- Yes — strong surplus above expenses (DSCR 1.35+) (Score: F=1.0)
- Yes — but it would be tight (DSCR 1.10–1.25) (Score: F=0.6)
- Possibly — depends on the amount (Score: F=0.35)
- No — little to no margin (Score: F=0.05)

### Q_R8: Banking Trajectory (Cash Balance Trend)

## Key Question Features

**Smart Grouping (No Redundancy):**
- Q_F3 collects start date (calculates business age automatically—never asked separately)
- Q_F10 collects all 3 credit scores at once (not asked one at a time)
- Q_F6 combines Account Type + Account Age + Daily Balance (three lender signals in one question)

**Conditional Logic (Reduces Cognitive Load):**
- Q_R16–Q_R18 only show if user has negative items (Q_R15="Yes")
- Q_F6 sub-fields only show if bank account selected (not if "No Bank Account")

**The +45 Boost:**
- Q_R12 (Past Business Loan Repayment) is the ONLY question with a score boost
- Answering "Yes — paid in full, on time" adds +45 points (most powerful single factor in system)
