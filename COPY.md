# COPY.md — FundReady Copy & Messaging Standards
# Read alongside CLAUDE.md every session

---

## Core Messaging Principle
People do not buy funding access.
They buy what funding access enables them to do.

People do not buy a score.
They buy clarity, confidence, and a better chance to move forward the right way.

---

## North Star Line
> "Write copy that makes the user feel informed enough to move, safe enough to trust, and motivated enough to continue."

---

## Primary Message
FundReady helps small business owners see how lenders see them, fix what matters most, and unlock better capital outcomes.

---

## The Line I Always Come Back To
"Whether you have been denied before or have never applied at all, FundReady helps you understand how lenders see your business before you risk the wrong move."

Or tighter: "Get funding-ready before you apply, or fix what held you back last time."

---

## Messaging Goal
All copy should make the user feel:
- this finally makes sense
- I can see where I stand
- I can fix this
- I do not have to guess anymore
- I can avoid costly mistakes
- I have a smarter path now

---

## Persona Copy Angles

### First-Time Strategic Applicant
Focus: do it right the first time · avoid preventable mistakes · know before you apply
Angle: "You have worked too hard to walk into the funding process blind. See how ready your business really is before you apply anywhere."

### Denied but Viable Owner
Focus: find out what blocked approval · fix the gaps · turn rejection into a roadmap
Angle: "Stop applying without a strategy. Find out exactly what blocked approval and fix it."

### Growth-Oriented Operator
Focus: graduate to better capital · larger amounts · better terms · bank and SBA readiness
Angle: "You are ready for better capital. Here is the path to get there."

### Underserved Founder
Focus: finally understand the rules · guided without judgment · turn uncertainty into progress
Angle: "Access a path that was never clearly explained to you. We'll walk it with you."

### Advisor / Channel Partner
Focus: structured client guidance · recurring value · less chaos
Angle: "Help clients get funding-ready with a system that works every time."

## Tone Rules

### Sound Like
- clear
- persuasive
- grounded
- emotionally intelligent
- calm
- confident
- trustworthy

### Never Sound Like
- robotic
- overhyped
- desperate
- gimmicky
- manipulative
- vague

---

## Writing Standards
- write at a strong middle-school to early high-school clarity level (customer-facing)
- prefer short paragraphs
- use concrete outcomes
- explain why something matters
- reduce shame and blame
- make the next action obvious
- use financial language carefully and clearly
- write as a guide, not a hustler

---

## CTA Philosophy
CTAs should feel: low-risk · clear · empowering · immediate · useful

**Good CTAs:**
- See where you stand
- Get your FundScore
- View your readiness profile
- Find your next best move
- See what is blocking approval
- Unlock your capital path

**Weak CTAs:**
- Apply Now
- Get Money
- Start Today (vague)
- Learn More

---

## Trust Language Signals
Use when appropriate:
- no credit pull required
- no bank login required
- results in minutes
- bank-grade security
- clear next steps
- personalized guidance
- built to help you prepare before applying

---

## Language to Never Use
- guaranteed approval
- instant funding success
- secret lender hack
- beat the banks
- get money fast no matter what
- everyone gets approved
- this changes everything forever

---

## Secondary Theme Lines

## Landing Page Formula
1. hidden problem (most owners don't know how lenders see them)
2. why most get it wrong (apply too early, wrong product, preventable gaps)
3. what FundReady reveals (your real readiness signal)
4. how FORGE guides improvement (prioritized actions, clear steps)
5. what outcomes unlock over time (better products, better rates, bigger amounts)
6. why this is safer than guessing (no credit pull, no obligation, real guidance)
7. CTA (see where you stand)

---

## FAQ Copy Priorities
Reduce friction around:
- credit pull concerns
- privacy concerns
- who this is for
- whether good credit is required
- whether this is only for denied businesses
- what makes this different from a broker
- what happens after the score

## Avoid Overusing
- AI buzzwords
- disruption clichés
- generic fintech jargon
- empty empowerment language

---

## In-Product Copy Standard
Every UI element, tooltip, section header, and empty state should answer:
1. What is this?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What should I do next?
4. What do I get for doing it?

---

## Guardrails
- never imply guaranteed approval
- never imply FundReady controls lender decisions
- never make unsupported compliance or legal claims
- never invent lender thresholds without marking them as illustrative
- never write copy that undermines trust or regulatory safety
- do not use fear as the primary motivator — use it as a prompt, not a hammer
